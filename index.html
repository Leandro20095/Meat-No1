The Sausage Making Process

  1. Prepare Ingredients and Equipment: Cut meat and fat into chunks suitable for the grinder. Chill the meat and your grinder parts (blade, plate, etc.) in the freezer for 1-2 hours; this keeps the fat cold and prevents smearing during grinding.
  2. First Grind: Using a coarse grinding plate, gradually feed the chilled meat and fat chunks into the machine's hopper. Grind all the ingredients into a large bowl.
  3. Seasoning and Mixing: Add all your pre-measured spices (salt, garlic, black pepper, etc.) and the iced water to the ground meat mixture. Mix thoroughly with your hands until well combined and the mixture becomes sticky.
  4. Taste Test (Optional but Recommended): Fry a small patty of the mixture in a skillet to taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary before stuffing the entire batch.
  5. Second Grind and Chilling: If a finer texture is desired, fit a finer plate onto your machine and push the meat mixture through a second time. Place the seasoned ground meat back in the fridge or freezer to chill again.
  6. Prepare Casings: While the meat chills, soak your natural casings in a bowl of warm water for at least an hour. Run water through them to rinse out any salt and ensure they are desalinated.
  7. Stuffing the Sausages: Equip your machine with the sausage stuffing nozzle. Thread the wet casing onto the nozzle, pushing it all the way to the base. Feed the meat mixture into the stuffer and start the machine slowly, guiding the casing off the nozzle as it fills.
  8. Form Links and Remove Air: Twist the long sausage into desired lengths, alternating the direction of the twist for each link to keep them secure. Use a sausage pricker or fine needle to prick any visible air bubbles to prevent them from bursting later.
  9. Cure/Store/Cook: The fresh sausages can be cooked on a grill, stored in the refrigerator for up to a week, or frozen for longer-term storage.
